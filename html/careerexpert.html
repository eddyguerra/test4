Discovery

Understanding the Problem

Audience

Individuals who are figuring out how to get into a specific career/ industry as quickly as possible. Those users will usually be between the ages of 18-35.


Need

A way of gaining easy access to industry veterans who will be able to impart knowledge and give actionable advice to the user.


Opportunity

To help the users connect with a community of like minded individuals and senior level career personnel, this will provide them with tools that will expedite their path to gaining a successful career.

User Research

Although this is the problem we have, I wanted to know if other people may have it too. I’ve collected quotes from users of career navigation services through user interviews that aligned with some initial ideas I have had for Career Expert:

Usability Testing

I tested the first prototype with 4 participants between the ages of 18-45.The goal is to study the learn-ability and usability of the app for new users. I will observe the users understanding and navigation of the app whiles they complete 4 core tasks. From the user testing and feedback, Affinity Maps were created categorizing findings into observations, positive quotes, negative quotes, and errors. I also created a Spreadsheet to better organize the information collected through affinity mapping to see clear visual patterns.



Issue 1 – Profile is not fitting of a social platform (High)

Suggested Change

To redesign the profile page in which users can view their social page and have a dropdown menu that holds settings.


Evidence

2 users pointed out that the profile was not fitting of a social platform in their opinion and would like it to have a similar framework to mainstream social media sites.

Prefrence Testing

A preference test was run with Usability Hub in order to test 2 designs each on 3 different app pages.

These app pages included:

  • Sign up page during Onboarding

  • Social page Hierarchy

  • Profile Page



Test 1 – Sign Up


Goal

Insight was needed on how Users proffered the visual hierarchy of buttons when user looks at someone’s profile page.

Comments

Users stated that this hierarchy is more pleasing to the eye which gives a good progression flow when looking at the page.

Conclusion

Users see Version B as more structural and less chaotic than the original. Version B also gives extra place to add more information when/if needed.

Test 3- Profile Page Menu


Goal

To decide whether users prefer the profile menu as its own page or as a drop down menu.

Comments

user commented that they believed when a the menu button is clicked that a overlaying menu is supposed to pop up while some users felt that the page felt more like an actual profile page.

Conclusion

Most users prefer some sort of overlay which gives a sense of familiarity while others prefer the menu to have a separate page for itself.

Style Guide

Once my revised iteration was complete, I cataloged all of Career Expert’s visual design elements in a style guide to achieve brand consistency with any future iterations. As the app continued to change and evolve, so did the style guide. I was able to change inconsistent colors, UI elements, iconography, etc. as the design process unfolded.
